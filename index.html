HUMAN BEINGS AND ENVIRONMENT

  • Response of living beings to changes in the environment; adaptation in plants and animals.
  • Modification of the environment by human beings to protect themselves against changes and meet their needs.
  • Effects of human activities and population growth on agriculture; harnessing of energy, housing, industrial development and other areas of consumption.
  • Consequence of human activities - stress on land use, water sources, energy and mineral resources, forest, ocean life; environmental degradation.

Clearing forests for human use adversely affects many plant and animal species, they may become extinct. Human beings have always been dependent on nature for meeting the needs of foods, clothing and shelter. There remained virtualy a harmonious relationship between men and nature until a few centuries ago. Scientific development and technological progress in the recent few centuries gave man the wrong notion that he was above nature and could become its master. Driven by these wrong notions, men began to use the natural resources thoughtlessly in order to achieve the following aims:

  • To fulfill the requirments of a multiplying human population.
  • To ensure protection adversities of nature.
  • To speed up development and progress.
  • To be called wealthy and civilised.
  • To satisfy his desire to have and use more and more resources.
